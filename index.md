## About me

Jeyong Sohn is an associate professor of Faculty of Law at Rikkyo University. His research interests are in the area of contemporary Japanese politics, comparative political finance, and Korean politics. 

He completed his B.A. in international relations at Seoul National University and received his M.A. and Ph.D. from the University of Tokyo. In his dissertation, titled “Policy Differentiation of Political Parties and the Political Finance Regulation,” he focused on how party competition brings about different types of political finance regime in democracies. 

His works include “The 2012 General Election and the 2013 House of Councilors Election: Where did the votes for the DPJ go (co-authored),” and “Settlement Without Consensus: International Pressure, Domestic Backlash, and the Comfort Women Issue in Japan (co-authored).”
